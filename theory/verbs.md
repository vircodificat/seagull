# Verbs

When a verb ends in -ing, you can add a `*G` stroke to inflect it.
When it ends in -ing, if there are no finals beyond `G`, you can fold in `*G` to inflect it.
This prevents conflicts between words like [doing](DO*G) and [dog](DOG) and between [ring](RING) and [ringing](RI*NG).

You add `-S` or `-D` to a verb to conjugate it.
For example, [love](LO*F) becomes [loves](LO*F) and [loved](LO*FD).
